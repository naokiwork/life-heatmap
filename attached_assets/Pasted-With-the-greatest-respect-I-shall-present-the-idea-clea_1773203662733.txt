Viral App Idea

Life Heatmap

A mobile and web application that visualizes your life activity like a GitHub contribution graph.

Instead of commits, the heatmap represents:
	•	studying
	•	coding
	•	workouts
	•	reading
	•	productivity
	•	any personal effort

The result is a visual map of your life progress.

People naturally want to share it on social media, which creates strong viral growth.

⸻

1. Core Concept

“Your life becomes a contribution graph.”

Example heatmap:

3. Main Features

Life Heatmap

GitHub-style visualization of daily effort.

Users see:
	•	weekly progress
	•	monthly heatmap
	•	yearly heatmap

⸻

Activity Categories

Examples:
	•	Coding
	•	Studying
	•	Workout
	•	Reading
	•	Meditation

	•	Business work
	•	Music practice

Session Timer

Start Coding

Streak System

Example:

	•	Duolingo
	•	Snapchat

⸻

Life Score

A calculated productivity score.

Example formula:

Life Score =
Study × 2
Coding × 2
Workout × 1.5
Reading × 1

AI Insights

The system analyzes behavior.

Example insights:

Your productivity peaks on Mondays.
You skip workouts on Fridays.

9. Monetization Strategy

Now to your important question.

Which is better: subscription or one-time purchase?

For this app, subscription is clearly better.

⸻

Why Subscription Is Better

This is a habit-tracking product.

Users use it every day.

Examples of similar apps:
	•	Strava → subscription
	•	Notion → subscription
	•	Duolingo → subscription
	•	GitHub → subscription

These products rely on ongoing value.

⸻

Recommended Pricing Model

Freemium model.

Free plan:
	•	Heatmap
	•	Basic tracking
	•	Streaks

Pro plan (subscription):
	•	AI insights
	•	Advanced analytics
	•	Unlimited categories
	•	Social leaderboard
	•	Custom themes

Price example:

$3 / month
or
$20 / year


⸻

When One-Time Purchase Works

One-time purchase works for apps that are:
	•	tools
	•	utilities
	•	calculators
	•	offline apps

Example:
	•	calculator
	•	camera app
	•	editing tool

But habit and productivity apps perform better with subscriptions.

⸻
